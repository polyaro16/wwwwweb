Winter-spring 1942
Since the beginning of this year, too, no changes. 20.2 I returned from Grebenka, stayed there for a few days, brought some food. Before that, the situation was disastrous. They set firm prices, and everything disappeared from the bazaar. Speculators began to take 12-15 rubles for a glass of millet, and it is impossible to buy it. Firm prices were canceled again. The imported food will not be enough for a long time.
Spring has come after a harsh winter. There are sunny good days. I dread to think of a new trip to the village, but it is necessary. I don't work anywhere. I need to get a job somewhere. It's still cold. I'm still going to go to the village. It will be even worse in the spring. I started working at the factory on 8.04, and on the same day I was summoned by a summons to Germany and my mother, too. The whole city is in an uproar, and all they're talking about is going to Germany.
